The "Conference Call" Trick:
Building a pocket looper that works on a smartphone browser without an audio interface or wired headphones comes with a unique challenge: preventing the microphone from hearing and recording the app's own backing track (audio bleed). WebLooper solves this by briefly fooling your smartphone into thinking you're on a speakerphone call while you're in the recording window. That is why you will notice a slightly "lo-fi" quality while recording. But when you hit "DONE" and return to the main screen, it releases the microphone and switches back to higher-quality audio.

The 1-Beat Spotlight Moat:
To further solve latency and audio bleed across various sluggish devices, WebLooper uses an extreme spotlight cycle. The backing track drops out precisely ONE beat before the recording window starts, and remains muted until ONE beat after it finishes. You are essentially left to keep time using the visual grid during recording. It guarantees zero audio bleed.
